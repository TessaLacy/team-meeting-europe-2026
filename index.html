Grand Place / Centre
Historic center · Most central
Most Central

The geographic heart of Brussels, steps from the iconic Grand Place and Brussels Central Station. Great restaurant variety and easy access to all neighborhoods. Ideal for exploring between meetings — and perfectly positioned for the team member arriving by train from London.

Grand Place Steps AwayGreat DiningAll Transport Links
Sainte-Catherine
Trendy · 5 min walk from center

A charming, walkable neighborhood minutes from the Grand Place. Famous for its seafood restaurants, lively square, and boutique hotels. Lower tourist density with a high safety rating — a great base for a team trip.

Boutique HotelsSeafood & DiningSafe & Walkable
De Brouckère / Rue Neuve
Shopping district · City centre north

Just north of the Grand Place along the vibrant Boulevard Adolphe Max, this area anchors Brussels' main shopping strip (Rue Neuve) and is steps from De Brouckère metro station. Lively, central, and full of dining options — about a 10-minute walk to the Grand Place.

De Brouckère MetroShopping & DiningCity Centre
Booking Tip

Check the EU Council calendar for summits around March 15–20 — hotel prices spike when sessions are in progress. Book early to lock in the best rates.

Innere Stadt (1st District)
Historic center · Business hub
Best for Business

Vienna's geographic and business center. The Stephansdom, Hofburg Palace, and most major corporate offices are all here. Most 4-star and 5-star business hotels are in this district.

Most CentralTop HotelsU-Bahn Access
Neubau (7th District)
Creative district · Local vibe

Vienna's coolest neighborhood — independent coffee shops, galleries, and excellent restaurants. Great mid-range hotels at lower prices than the 1st District. Connected by U3 metro.

Arts & CultureGreat CafesU3 Metro
Wieden (4th District)
Quiet · Residential · Authentic

A predominantly local neighborhood with very few tourists. Beautiful historic buildings and cozy cafes make it a great base for a working trip. Connected via U1, U2, and trams.

Local & AuthenticBudget-FriendlyMultiple Trams
Mariahilf (6th District)
Shopping & dining · Walkable

Mariahilferstrasse — Vienna's main shopping street — runs right through here. Tons of mid-range hotel options and walking distance to the major sights in the 1st District.

Shopping AccessMid-Range HotelsWalkable to Center
Booking Tip

Stay within the Gürtel (ring boulevard) — districts 1 through 9. The 6th or 7th districts offer great savings without sacrificing access. Vienna's U-Bahn is fast and clean, so a few stops from center is no hardship.

Mid-March is shoulder season in Prague — low prices and high availability. Vinohrady is 20–30% cheaper than Old Town with comparable quality. Note: Chicago and St. Pete, FL will need connecting flights via Frankfurt or Amsterdam.
